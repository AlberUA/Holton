Types of containers

Dry cargo container is a standard shipping container utilized for the transportation of general goods that do not necessitate specific temperature regulation or ventilation. It is a versatile and extensively employed container type engineered to safely carry a wide range of merchandise, such as dry goods, packaged items, machinery, and other non-perishable products.
Open-top container is a shipping container with an open roof, specifically designed to accommodate cargo that surpasses the height restrictions of standard containers or requires access from above using a crane. It is an ideal solution for transporting oversized or bulky items, such as machinery, construction materials, and other goods that cannot be conveniently loaded through the regular doors of a closed container. The open-top design allows for effortless loading and unloading processes, ensuring efficiency and making it a preferred option for specific types of cargo.
Flatracks come in 20' and 40' dimensions and are designed to handle various types of cargo, including heavy-lifts, overheight or overwidth shipments, as well as oddly shaped and oversized loads such as machinery, cables, drums, metal sheets, steel coils, heavy vehicles, timber, forest products, and even yachts.
Refrigerated container is a specialized type of shipping container that provides temperature-controlled storage and transportation for perishable goods. It is designed with built-in refrigeration systems to maintain specific temperature ranges, typically ranging from -30째C to +30째C (-22째F to +86째F), ensuring the preservation and quality of sensitive cargo like fruits, vegetables, pharmaceuticals, and other temperature-sensitive products. The reefer container is equipped with insulation, ventilation, and temperature control mechanisms to regulate the internal environment and prevent spoilage or damage to the goods during transit.
Bulk containers are large, sturdy containers specifically designed for the transportation and storage of loose or granular materials in bulk quantities. These containers are commonly used for transporting commodities such as grains, minerals, chemicals, liquids, powders, and other bulk goods. They feature a robust construction and are capable of withstanding the weight and pressure exerted by the bulk cargo.
A tank container, also known as an ISO tank container, is a specialized type of shipping container that is designed to transport liquid or gaseous cargo. Tank containers are commonly used in industries such as chemical, food and beverage, pharmaceutical, and oil and gas, where the transportation of bulk liquids or gases is required. They provide a cost-effective and secure solution for the transportation of bulk liquid and gas shipments by sea.
